How to get Started

To get into the activity of airsoft, you need to have a understanding of your ability to run around a field in your equipment while looking out for enemy players.

Required equipment

  • Face Protection
  • BB's
  • Weapons
  • Batteries
  • Green Gas, CO2

Standard Rules

Every field has it's own set of rules, but here are some regulations that organized locations should have;

  1. Keep your mask on when you're on the field
  2. If hit by a BB, call yourself out and exit the field
  3. Do not use a weapon on the fully-automatic settings if in close quarters
  4. Guns should shoot at the appropriate speeds for the environment
  5. Don't start physical fights over toy guns.

The thing about a Sportsmanship rule

Though it is a rule that if you get shot you are out, since the pellets normally do not leave marks, it is very simple to cheat. Simply pretending not to feel a BB is a common practice, and there is an equally effective way to counteract cheating. Unless the individual puts their hand up or calls themselves out, just keep shooting. Though it is easy to brush off one BB hitting them, not many people can get hit twenty times without flinching. It seems like a harsh punishment, but as long they don't call themselves out, they are still a player who needs to be hit.
